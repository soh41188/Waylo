Why this happens
The pricing logic airlines use
01
Airlines price routes, not legs
When you search BSB → LIS as one trip, the airline's revenue management system prices it as a single origin-destination market (O&D). That BSB–LIS market has its own demand curve, competitor fares, and yield targets — completely separate from the BSB–GRU or GRU–LIS markets.
BSB → LIS $700 (O&D market: transatlantic)
BSB → GRU $89 (O&D market: domestic Brazil)
GRU → LIS $411 (O&D market: GRU–Europe hub)
02
Hub premiums inflate bundled fares
The GRU–LIS route is operated as a hub-to-hub long haul. When you buy the full BSB–LIS bundle, the airline applies its long-haul pricing to the entire ticket. But GRU–LIS sold alone is cheaper because it's competing with TAP, Air France, and Iberia on that specific leg — driving the price down independently.
GRU → LIS bundled ~$611 (implied in $700 bundle)
GRU → LIS separate $411 (competing with 4 airlines)
Difference: $200 on a single leg
03
How Waylo detects the gap
Waylo queries the airline (or flight API) for both the bundled fare and each individual leg simultaneously. It then sums the legs and compares. If the sum of legs is lower than the bundle, it surfaces the split — along with the exact booking steps and the risk disclaimer.
query: BSB→LIS one-ticket → $700
query: BSB→GRU leg-only → $89
query: GRU→LIS leg-only → $411
sum of legs: $500
split_saving = 700 - 500 = $200 ✓ surface this
04
Search each leg individually — not as a connecting flight
This is the most important step and where most people get tripped up. On the airline's own website, if you search "BSB to LIS" you get the bundled price. You need to search BSB to GRU as a standalone trip, then separately search GRU to LIS as a standalone trip. Do not use "add connecting flight" — that rebundles the pricing.
✗ latam.com → "BSB to LIS" → $700
✓ latam.com → "BSB to GRU" (standalone) → $89
✓ latam.com → "GRU to LIS" (standalone) → $411
⚠️ Know the risks before you split
  • These are two separate bookings with different confirmation numbers. The airline has no legal obligation to rebook you on Leg 2 if Leg 1 is delayed.
  • Build in a generous layover — at least 2–3 hours for domestic connections, 3–4 hours for international. Waylo flags if your layover is too tight.
  • Check baggage rules. On separate tickets you may need to collect and re-check your luggage at the layover airport, even on the same airline.
  • Travel insurance can cover a missed connection on separate tickets. Waylo will suggest a policy at checkout.
  • Prices on individual legs can change between the time you check Leg 1 and the time you book Leg 2. Book both in the same session.
